Big data is going to impact your career in the years ahead. Technology professionals will struggle to build infrastructure to process larger and larger data sets, while business analysts and data scientists will have to adapt their methods and algorithms to cope. Meanwhile, statisticians and computer scientists are pushing the edge of what's possible by building more powerful tools to turn data into insights.

The MinneAnalytics Big Data Tech conference is your chance to learn about big data right here in Minnesota. We'll feature dozens of leaders in the field, from around the region and around the country. Vendors will display bleeding-edge versions of their products with onsite tutorials. And, most importantly, you'll be able to learn from your peers in the Twin Cities about the tools they find valuable and what they're planning for the future.

Tickets will be free, but will sell out fast. So be sure to subscribe to our email list or follow us on Twitter for updates!

Learn

We'll have presentations on the latest new big data technologies, including:

  • Apache Spark
  • NoSQL databases including Cassandra, Couchbase, and MarkLogic
  • Infrastructure components including Docker, OpenStack, and AWS
  • Machine learning and data mining algorithms

Our 2016 speaker list is not yet available, but see our 2015 schedule to learn about the kinds of speakers we'll feature.

Network

With headquarters for many of the world's largest corporations, the Twin Cities area is a global hub for analytics and data science. At Big Data Tech, you'll be able to:

  • Meet your peers in analytics at other regional powerhouses
  • Meet vendor representatives and find out about their latest products
  • Be the first to hear about analytics job openings

And do it all without leaving the Twin Cities!

Location

The conference will be on June 7, 2016, at Normandale Community College's Partnership Center.

Admission will be free for individuals (a fee will apply for recruiters).
